Carlos Lozada is a Peruvian-American journalist and the nonfiction book critic of The Washington Post. He won the 2019 Pulitzer Prize for Criticism and was a finalist for the prize in 2018. The Pulitzer Board cited his "trenchant and searching reviews and essays. . . on government and the American experience." He also received the 2015 National Book Critics Circle Nona Balakian Citation for Excellence in Reviewing.

Lozada was born in Lima, Peru, and migrated to California with his family as a child. He late returned to Peru, where he lived until completing high school. He earned a bachelor's degree in economics and political science from the University of Notre Dame in 1993. In 1997, he graduated from the Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs at Princeton University with a master's degree in public administration. After graduation, Lozada worked as an economic analyst at the Federal Reserve Bank of Atlanta in Atlanta, Georgia.

    • He joined the staff of The Washington Post in 2005 and served as economics editor, national security editor and Outlook editor.
In 1999, Lozada moved to Washington, D.C. and became an editor of Foreign Policy, eventually becoming the magazine's managing editor. Lozada was a 2004–2005 Knight-Bagehot Fellow in Business and Economics Journalism at Columbia University in New York. He joined the staff of The Washington Post in 2005 and served as economics editor, national security editor and Outlook editor. He became the paper's nonfiction book critic in 2015.
Lozada joined the University of Notre Dame Faculty in 2009 as an adjunct professor for the Washington Program. He teaches a class on American Political Journalism.
